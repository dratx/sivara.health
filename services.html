Specialised Orthopaedic Procedures

Our single-specialty focus allows us to provide unparalleled depth of expertise in a range of elective orthopaedic surgeries.

Total hip replacement is one of the most successful procedures in modern medicine, designed to relieve pain and restore mobility in those with severe hip joint damage.

Who is it for? This procedure is life-changing for patients whose daily lives are limited by hip pain and stiffness, commonly caused by osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, or previous injury.

How does it work? The surgeon removes the damaged "ball and socket" of the hip joint and replaces them with durable, high-quality artificial components (implants). Our partner surgeons in Thailand are experts in modern techniques, including minimally-invasive approaches that can lead to less muscle disruption and a potentially faster recovery. The result is a new, smoothly functioning joint, allowing you to walk, climb stairs, and live with dramatically reduced pain.
A total knee replacement resurfaces the knee joint, which has been damaged by arthritis or injury, to alleviate pain and restore function and stability.

Who is it for? This is a proven solution for patients with chronic knee pain, instability, and loss of motion that no longer responds to conservative treatments like medication or physiotherapy.

How does it work? The goal is not to replace the entire knee, but to resurface the damaged parts. The surgeon removes worn-out cartilage and bone from the end of the thigh bone and top of the shin bone, replacing them with high-quality metal and medical-grade plastic implants. Our partner hospitals are at the forefront of utilising advanced robotic-assisted technology for this procedure. This allows the surgeon to create a precise 3D model of your unique anatomy for a more accurate implant fit, which can lead to a more natural feel and a smoother post-operative recovery.
We provide access to elite neurosurgeons and orthopaedic spine specialists for procedures designed to relieve pain and restore function caused by spinal conditions.

Who is it for? Patients suffering from chronic, often debilitating, pain from conditions like herniated discs (causing sciatica) or spinal stenosis (a narrowing of the spinal canal that presses on nerves).

How does it work? The specific procedure depends on your condition. For example:
• A Discectomy involves the surgeon carefully removing the portion of a herniated disc that is pressing on a nerve, relieving pain, numbness, or weakness.
• A Spinal Fusion is designed to eliminate painful motion by permanently joining two or more vertebrae, creating a single, solid bone. These are highly specialised procedures, and our role is to connect you with the right expert at a world-class, JCI-accredited facility to ensure the highest standard of care.
Our network includes top surgeons who specialise in repairing joint and soft tissue damage, helping active individuals recover from injury and get back to the sports and activities they love.

Who is it for? This includes patients with common but painful conditions such as a torn rotator cuff in the shoulder, joint instability, or a ruptured Anterior Cruciate Ligament (ACL) in the knee.

How does it work? Many of these repairs are performed using arthroscopy, a minimally invasive "keyhole" technique. The surgeon uses a tiny camera and specialised instruments through small incisions to repair the damage. This approach often leads to less pain, reduced scarring, and a faster return to function compared to traditional open surgery. Whether you need a shoulder replacement or an ACL reconstruction, our partners provide expert care designed to restore your strength and stability.
Our network of elite orthopaedic specialists covers the full spectrum of musculoskeletal care, including complex conditions affecting the hands, wrists, feet, and ankles.

Who is it for? This is for patients experiencing pain, deformity, numbness, or loss of function from specific conditions such as advanced arthritis in smaller joints, severe bunions, or nerve compression syndromes like carpal tunnel.

How does it work? Our partners perform a wide array of specialised surgeries with precision and care. For example, Carpal Tunnel Release alleviates pressure on the nerve in the wrist to relieve hand numbness and pain, while Bunion Correction realigns the joint at the base of the big toe to restore foot comfort and function. Whether you require an ankle fusion, elbow surgery, or another specific procedure, our extensive network ensures access to the right expert.

If your condition isn't listed here, we encourage you to reach out. Our physician-led team can assess your needs during a free consultation and connect you with the appropriate sub-specialist in Thailand.
